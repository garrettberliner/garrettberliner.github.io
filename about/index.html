About Me

Hello 👋😄!

I'm Garrett, a Technical Program / Product Manager at Microsoft working on improving developer productivity through AI-forward approaches 🚀. Currently, I lead cross-organizational programs & products within Windows + Devices that help developers work more efficiently with AI to empower over 1.2 billion monthly active Windows users 🤖. Before Microsoft, I spent 4 years conducting Computing Education Research at Oregon State University under Dr. Jennifer Parham-Mocello, co-authoring 4 double-blind peer-reviewed publications & defending 2 theses. We investigated innovative practices to make CS more accessible through game-oriented curriculum & empowering K-12 CS teachers 🎓📚.

Outside of work, I love exploring the Pacific Northwest 🏔️, experimenting with cooking (especially Latin American recipes passed down from my Mom) 🍲, sustainability & upcycling projects ♻️, hosting board game nights with friends🎲, & playing soccer ⚽ or rock climbing 🧗‍♂️. Through & through, I'm passionate about finding ways to make computing more equitable & accessible for everyone 🌎.

GCCUT: Oregon State University

Completed alongside M.S. in Computer Science · 18 credits · 2023–2025

The Graduate Certificate in College & University Teaching (GCCUT) is a graduate-level program that prepares students for instructional roles in higher education. This interdisciplinary certificate develops skills in course design, evidence-based pedagogy, equitable instruction, & reflective teaching practice - qualifying recipients to teach at the college & university level.
